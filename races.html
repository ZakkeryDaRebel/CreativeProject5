Which D&D Race should you choose?

The Races

Races are a very cool decision to make because there are tons of different races to choose from. Each race gives some abilities and skills along with giving some boosts to some abilities. So the best way to decide what Race to choose from is 1. What Race do you think is cool or what Race do you want to play the most, and 2. What Race is the best for your class. If your Race gives a boost to the Primary Ability of your class, then overall, its a good choice. For the most part, Races don't change much on how you play, so don't stress about your decision and pick something you'll enjoy playing.

Dwarf

Dwarfs are stout and hardy. Though they may be on the smaller end, that doesn't mean they are any less capable. Used to living underground, they have a natural distrust towards anyone not of their own, but it causes them to have strong bonds for all people in their clan. Once you earn their trust, they're loyal to you till the end. Dwarfs boost Constitution, and can boost Wisdom or Strength.

Elf

Elves are slender and graceful. They live a long time which gives them a very different outlook on time then most other people. Used to living in the wildlife and the forests, they are one with nature, and use their seclusion to master their skills. Elves boost Dexterity, and can boost Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma.

Halfling

Halflings are kind and curious, yet small and practical. You may know them as Hobbits. They're small Humans that take joy in the pleasures around them. Usually from a peaceful community, and ones that always love a good tale. But they do travel occasionally and generally welcome anywhere as their hard work and loyalty draw other's appreciation. Halflings boost Dexterity, and can boost Charisma or Constitution.

Human

Humans are, well, you should know, very diverse. Humans are one the Race with the shortest lifespan, but other races are pretty impressed what they have been capable of during their lifespan. They are very versatile and can accomplish a lot of different things as long as they put their time to it. Humans can get a boost in any or all Ability.

Dragonborn

Dragonborns are sadly not real dragons with wings, but more of a mix between humans and dragons. They have been been shapped either by draconic gods or the dragons themselves, they hatch from eggs in a unique race, combining the best attributes of dragons and humanoids. They grow up in a Dragonborn clan and they take care of each other as the world guards them with caution and sometimes fear. Dragonborns boost Strength and Charisma.

Gnome

Gnomes are not really related to the garden gnomes you place in your yard to scare away animals. These Gnomes take pleasure with being alive and just want to do anything and everything while they are still capable of it. They usually live in hilly wooded lands, and sometimes in underground and hide their homes from unwelcome visitors. Gnomes boost Intelligence, and can boost Dexterity or Constitution.

Half-Elf

Half-Elves are a mixture of Humans and Elves. Being half of each, they don't really fit into either society, as the Elves seem Half-Elves as unpure, and Humans see them as Elves. This is what makes most Half-Elves to become Wanderers or Diplomants, either dedicating themselves to traveling between different societies or choosing seclusion and isolation. Half-Elves boost Charisma and any other Ability.

Half-Orc

Half-Orcs are a mixture of Humans and Orcs. Being half Orc, they are usually feared from their Orc heritage. They are scarred and strong, taking pride in their battle scars and striving to achieve greatness for their mighty deeds and recognizition for their barbaric customs and savage fury. Most Half-Orcs live among Orcs, or Humans, as other Races don't accept them. Half-Orcs boost Strength and Constiution.

Tiefling

Tieflings are derived from Human bloodlines, however their infernal and demonic heritage clearly shows, tainting their appearance. They were originally created as a Human struck a deal with Asmodeous, the overlord of the Nine Hells, giving them strong powers at the expense of every other race wary of them and try and cast them out. And so, they usually are self-reliant and suspicious of others betraying them. Tieflings boost Charisma and Intelligence.
